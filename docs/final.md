# MIZAN – Update Assessment

## Executive Summary

The recent updates significantly improve MIZAN's scientific credibility and defensibility.

The project has evolved from a promising concept into a much stronger evidence-based system that is harder for technical judges to challenge during Q&A.

The biggest improvement is not the technology itself—it is the shift toward making scientifically supportable claims.

---

# Major Improvements

## 1. From "Illegal Well Detection" to "Inspection Prioritization"

### Previous Claim
> MIZAN detects illegal wells.

### Problem
A satellite cannot determine whether a well is legally licensed.

A judge could immediately ask:

> "How do you know this well is illegal?"

This creates a major validity risk.

### Updated Claim
> MIZAN identifies locations with a high probability of unauthorized irrigation that should be prioritized for inspection.

### Result

This transforms MIZAN from a legal enforcement tool into a decision-support system.

**Impact:** 10/10

---

## 2. Rainfall-Anomaly Logic

### Previous Logic

Green vegetation = suspicious.

### Problem

Not all green vegetation indicates unauthorized water use.

### Updated Logic

- Vegetation remains green.
- Rainfall is near zero.
- Vegetation remains active over time.
- Therefore water is likely being supplied artificially.

### Result

The inference becomes significantly stronger and more defensible.

**Impact:** 9/10

---

## 3. Proper Use of GRACE Data

### Previous Risk

Using GRACE to claim groundwater decline at the basin level.

### Problem

GRACE spatial resolution is too coarse for detailed basin-scale analysis.

A remote-sensing expert could challenge this immediately.

### Updated Position

GRACE is used as a regional evidence layer showing long-term groundwater depletion trends across Jordan and the Levant.

### Result

Scientific credibility increases substantially.

**Impact:** 10/10

---

## 4. Government Enforcement Evidence

### Improvement

Adding official statistics regarding:

- Illegal wells sealed
- Water saved through enforcement
- National groundwater management efforts

### Result

The project now demonstrates that:

1. The problem is real.
2. Authorities already spend resources addressing it.
3. Better targeting can create measurable value.

**Impact:** 9/10

---

## 5. Historical Case Validation

### Improvement

Comparing MIZAN outputs against known enforcement regions or documented violations.

### Result

Even a single successful historical validation dramatically improves confidence in the methodology.

This is one of the strongest possible improvements to the Validity criterion.

**Impact:** 9/10

---

# Remaining Weaknesses

## 1. Water-Loss Estimation Methodology

Any estimate of water extraction must be transparent.

Judges may ask:

> "How was this number calculated?"

The methodology should clearly include:

- Cultivated area
- Crop coefficients
- Evapotranspiration estimates
- Irrigation demand calculations
- Data sources

### Recommendation

Document every equation and source.

---

## 2. False Positives

A judge will likely ask:

> "What if the farm is operating legally?"

### Recommended Response

MIZAN does not:

- Declare violations
- Issue fines
- Replace inspectors

MIZAN does:

- Detect anomalies
- Prioritize inspections
- Reduce search costs
- Improve enforcement efficiency

---

## 3. Quantified National Impact

A stronger impact statement is still needed.

Example:

> If MIZAN helps authorities identify even a small percentage of unauthorized extraction, Jordan could potentially save millions of cubic meters of water annually.

### Recommendation

Develop a conservative impact model supported by public data.

# Overall Verdict

The updates substantially improve the project's scientific rigor and judge defensibility.

The biggest achievement is that MIZAN is no longer making claims that are easy to attack.

The project now positions itself as:

> A satellite-powered decision-support system that helps Jordanian authorities prioritize inspections and recover water resources more efficiently.

This framing is scientifically credible, operationally realistic, and highly aligned with AstroCode evaluation criteria.

**Overall Score: 9.0–9.5 / 10**

High, provided that:

1. Validation examples are completed.
2. Water-loss calculations are documented.

> A persistently green agricultural area during a rainless Jordanian summer is likely consuming water from an external source.
